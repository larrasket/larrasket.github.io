Monday, 24 October 2022

I was reading this fine article by Paul Graham http://paulgraham.com/avg.html when he said:

Robert and I both knew Lisp well, and we couldn't see any reason not to trust our instincts and go with Lisp. We knew that everyone else was writing their software in C++ or Perl. But we also knew that that didn't mean anything. If you chose technology that way, you'd be running Windows. When you choose technology, you have to ignore what other people are doing, and consider only what will work the best.

Wisdom of soul. But I laughed a lot of how Windows was notorious back then.

Edit: I'm still browsing his old essaies and just found another one:

People frightened by Lisp make up other reasons for not using it. The standard excuse, back when C was the default language, was that Lisp was too slow. Now that Lisp dialects are among the faster languages available, that excuse has gone away. Now the standard excuse is openly circular: that other languages are more popular.

(Beware of such reasoning. It gets you Windows.)

http://www.paulgraham.com/iflisp.html

Monday, 03 October 2022

Nobility versus pleasure—. I believe that the possession of a kitten instead of a dog is very moronic act, although it occurs via humanistic polarizers; Cats look very cute, smell like babies, they even have eyes as wide as children's. Which all are convincing reasons for us —human beings— to adore that kind. However, cats are felidae, individual creatures that never live in communities, thus they cannot provide any emotional feelings toward its owner. That's why I find YouTube videos about cat reaction to \(x\) very idiotic, the cat mostly doesn't know that the so-called partner exists. In the other hand, dogs were domesticated, and before they always lived in communities henceforth they are very emotional creatures. They can be sad, they can feel love, etc. However, I think that the comparison of cats vs. dogs is vastly interesting because cats are tremendously hard to tame, dogs may not need to. It'd be such a patrician act to approve to that challenge in my opinion.
