Computer Science Books

I do not like to talk about professional stuff here, but I was asked frequently about books on cretin topics related to the Computer Science field, so I decided to list the textbooks I used in my scholarly. Of course, lots of them I did not 'finish', the but they are favorable references to me.

Fundamental Books

Those are mostly not books of 'recommendations', at least in my opinion. Those are the must-read books or, must known, references and books that any computer scientist should read (or crash with).

  • Logic (Nicholas J. Smith)
  • Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs
  • Discrete and Combinatorial Mathematics (Grimaldi)
  • Calculus (Spivak)
  • The Art of Unix Programming (Raymond)
  • Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces (Arpaci-Dusseau et. al)
  • Computer Algebra and Polynomials (Guiterrez)
  • Operating System Concepts (Silberschatz)
  • The Art of Computer Programming
  • Data Structures and Algorithm Analysis (Shaffer)
  • An Introduction to Genetic Algorithms (Mitchell)
  • Speech and Language Processing (Jurafsky et. al)
  • Computer Organization and Design (Patterson et. al)
  • Fundamentals of Computer Graphics Peter (Shirley)
  • Digital Signal Processing (Proakis et. al)
  • Signals and Systems (Ghosh)
  • The Art of Concurrency (Breshears)
  • Mathematical Theory of Communication (Claude Shannon)
  • Introduction to Data Comparison (Sayood)
  • Database System Concepts by Abraham Silberschatz
  • Foundation of Cryptography (Goldreich)
